What is Pan-Africanism??


The essence of Pan-Africanism is that all people on the African continent share a common cause and common destiny. In its overall context, Pan-Africanism aims at integrating all individuals of African descent, providing them a platform to peacefully unite for a common cause on the continent. As a result, there is need to implement policies and structures that help the continent progress towards the common goal. These structures include free trade, open borders, common markets, common currency and perhaps an African passport for all African nations. Many post-colonial African leaders were proponents of Pan-Africanism, including Kwame Nkrumah and later Muammar Gaddafi. The idea of Pan-Africanism allows African nations to unite, share and exchange resources within the continent while promoting peace and tranquility in the continent.
